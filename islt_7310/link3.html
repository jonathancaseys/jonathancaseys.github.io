Cymatics: Visualization of Sound



Students will gain a better understanding of the properties of sound waves through the use of cymatics, or visualizations of sound. This can be done through various mediums, such as chladni plates, water, magnetic liquids, fire, and electricity. In the case of a chladni plate, for example, a steel plate is connected to a tone generator. By covering the plate with sand or salt, the experimenter adjusts the tone generator until a resonant frequency is found. When this happens, the particles will form geometric patterns on the plate, as seen in this image to the left. The more complex the pattern, the higher the pitch, and the less complex, the lower the pitch. Watch this video here to see how cymatic patterns are used to show the properties of sound waves
