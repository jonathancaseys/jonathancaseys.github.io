Let's Learn about Sound: Properties of Strings


It can be common for young students to associate small instruments with "low" sounds, and large instruments with "high" sounds, simply because sometimes we associate low with small and high with big. But if we know any basics about the properties of strings, then we can learn a little bit about the relationship between the qualities of a string and its pitch.

In the infographic I have created to the left, we can see a graphic representation of a "standing wave", or the type of sound wave created by a string instrument. We can see that it moves back and forth, shaking widely at some points and narrowly at others. The next image shows a zoomed in ukuele, where we can see the vibrations but not specifically the back and forth motion that we saw in the first lesson with the slow-motion camera.
So why do some instruments make higher sounds and other instruments lower sounds? It heavily depends on the length of the strings. A long instrument, like the string bass in the picture, makes very low sounds, while the violin, with its short strings, makes very high sounds.
And then we have instruments that make both high and low sounds, like the autoharp in the bottom left.
